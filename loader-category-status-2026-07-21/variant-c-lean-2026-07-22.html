LEAN is the canonical design. Loaders chose it over the dense variant in the A/B review, so all further iteration happens here; dense remains a reference-only "showcase everything" version for design review. A lean row is tag # + short spec; category is the coloured left edge, upstream state is the corner dot, load status is the section header, and everything else (full spec, big shape, attribution, photo, per-tag actions) lives one tap away in the drawer. Finished work collapses to a "✓ N loaded" line. Tap the open rows (#42, #39) to see a drawer.

Frames 8–9 — partial close-out + carry forward. The scan is the assignment: whatever is loaded ships, whatever isn't stays in the bin for the next truck. The supervisor doesn't plan a split — they ratify an early close-out with a typed reason ("Split — carry forward"), still backed by the note + photo. The next truck simply reopens the run at 15 of 22, with the 7 remaining marked ↩ carry. No new operation, no reassignment, nothing for the loader to learn.
